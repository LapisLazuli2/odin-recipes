Okonomiyaki

Description

Okonomiyaki is a Japanese Teppanyaki, savoury pancake dish consisting of wheat flour batter and other ingredients cooked on a teppan. Common additions include cabbage, meat, and seafood, and toppings include okonomiyaki sauce, aonori, katsuobushi, Japanese mayonnaise, and pickled ginger.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plain flour)
  • ¼ tsp kosher salt
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¾ cup dashi
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup tenkasu/agedama
  • ¼ cup pickled red ginger
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • ½ lb sliced pork belly
  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Dried bonito flakes

Steps

The Batter

  1. In a large bowl, combine all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, and baking powder and mix all together.
  2. Peel and grate the nagaimo in a small bowl
  3. Add the grated the nagaimo and dashi to the bowl.
  4. Mix it all together until combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour.

Preparing the other ingredients

  1. Discard the core of the cabbage and then mince the cabbage leaves.
  2. Cut the pork belly slices in half and set aside.

Cook the okonomiyaki

  1. After one hour, take out the batter from the refrigerator. Add eggs, tempura scraps (tenkasu/agedama), and pickled red ginger (kizami beni shoga) to the bowl. Mix until combined.
  2. Add the minced cabbage to the batter, one-third of it at a time. Mix well before adding the rest.
  3. In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil on medium heat, and spread the batter in a circle on the pan.
  4. Place 2-3 slices of pork belly on top of the okonomiyaki and cook covered for 5 minutes.
  5. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip it over.
  6. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix its shape and keep it together. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Flip it over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes.

Adding the toppings

  1. Spread the okonomi sauce on top with a brush or spoon.
  2. Drizzle with Japanese mayonnaise in a zigzag pattern.
  3. Sprinkle with dried bonito flakes.
